Brazil - Pesticide and Honey Production
================
Luiz Felipe Martucci

Honey bees have a reputation for working hard. They not only produce
honey but pollinate flowering plants. Together with other pollinators,
they are responsible for more than 87% of flowering plant species’
reproduction (Ostiguy et al. (2019)). However, we are working hard to
cause bee losses. From 1966 to 1979, most bee losses were attributable
to pesticides (Rişcu and Bura (2013)), especially pesticides applied to
agricultural or horticultural landscapes (Ostiguy et al. (2019)).
Ignoring their impact on bee lives, Brazil has been introducing new and
buying a staggering amount of pesticides year after year.

Resources (IBAMA) shows that after Jair Bolsonaro’s election in 2018,
pesticide purchases increased drastically. Between 2018 and 2021

The data about honey production is from the Municipal Livestock Research
collected by the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE),
and data about approval of new pesticides is from the Brazilian Ministry
of Agriculture and Livestock. Our analysis will target the converging
timestamp in both datasets: 2000-2021.
